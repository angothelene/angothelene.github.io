About Me

I am a CNRS Research Scientist at the Institute of Environmental Geosciences in Grenoble, France.

My research focuses on the impact of global changes on the cycle of contaminants such as mercury and microplastics. I lead research projects in remote regions of the world, including the Arctic, the French Alps, and the Southern Ocean, using both field observations and modeling tools. My goal is to understand and mitigate the environmental risks posed by these contaminants in vulnerable ecosystems.

Feel free to reach out for collaborations!

Research Projects

Mercury Dynamics in the Arctic

Role: Principal Investigator

Description: Investigating the transport and transformation of mercury in Arctic ecosystems, with a focus on atmospheric deposition and its impact on local food webs.

Duration: 2022–2026

Microplastics in Alpine Lakes

Role: Co-Investigator

Description: Assessing the presence and sources of microplastics in high-altitude lakes of the French Alps, and their potential ecological consequences.

Duration: 2021–2025

Southern Ocean Contaminant Cycle

Role: Lead Researcher

Description: Studying the long-range transport of pollutants to the Southern Ocean and their accumulation in marine organisms.

Duration: 2020–2024
